## Supplementary: Page-Level Annotations

### Information Architecture Logic

The page follows a classic persuasion arc:

1. **Hero** — State the promise (what you get)
2. **Social proof bar** — Validate the problem exists (market-scale stats)
3. **Problem** — Make the pain vivid (why current options fail)
4. **Solution** — Present the relief (how we fix it)
5. **How it works** — Make it feel easy (reduce perceived effort)
6. **Benefits** — Deepen the value (five specific advantages)
7. **Comparison** — Prove superiority (side-by-side evidence)
8. **Pricing** — Address cost anxiety (transparent tiers + early access hook)
9. **FAQ** — Handle remaining objections (eight specific concerns)
10. **Final CTA** — Close with conviction (summarize + convert)

Each section advances one argument. No section tries to do everything. The page builds progressively from "here's what's broken" to "here's how we fix it" to "here's what it costs" to "sign up."

### Voice Consistency Notes

- Headlines are short (under 12 words where possible)
- Body copy uses simple words: "runs" not "executes," "picks" not "selects"
- Every competitive claim cites a specific, verifiable source (Snyk, Kaspersky, Cisco, etc.)
- Humor is dry and factual, never forced ("0 malware. That's the feature.")
- No exclamation points anywhere on the page
- No buzzwords from the avoid list (revolutionary, disruptive, game-changing, innovative, cutting-edge, leverage, synergy)
- The word "AI-powered" never appears (everything is AI-powered now)

### Audience-Specific Hooks

| Audience | Where they'll feel most engaged |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| OpenClaw refugees | Problem section (recognition), Comparison table (validation), FAQ Q1 and Q6 (direct answers) |
| AI-curious developers | Hero headline C (30 seconds vs 3 days), How It Works (simplicity), Benefits 2 and 4 (speed + multi-model) |
| Non-technical founders | Hero subheadline ("running in your browser"), Solution section ("no terminal, no dependencies"), Pricing (clear tiers), FAQ Q3 (data safety) |
